Before blogs had dashboards and tweets had threads, there was an email address that turned your messages into a blog. This is its story.

2008
The Beginning
Garry Tan and Yuri S. launch Posterous from a San Francisco apartment. The premise was audacious: what if your blog was just an email address?
2009
Viral Growth
"Post by email" becomes a movement. No CMS, no login, no friction. Photographers, writers, and everyday people embrace the simplicity. The team raises funding and builds a company around this radical idea.
2011
The Twitter Acquisition
Twitter acquires Posterous for an estimated $10M. The team joins Twitter, and Posterous enters a slow sunset. Millions of posts, memories, and stories left in limbo.
2013
The End
Posterous Spaces shuts down. Users scramble to export their content. A cautionary tale of platform dependencyâ€”but also a testament to the power of simple, elegant ideas.
2025
The Resurrection
In the age of AI agents, the email-as-interface idea finds new purpose. Clawederous revives the philosophy: your inbox as a command bus, your email as automation.

Why Email Still Matters

Posterous understood something profound: email is the universal protocol. It's everywhere, it works everywhere, and everyone knows how to use it. Clawederous carries that torch into the agent era.

Universal Access

No new apps, no accounts, no lock-in. Email is the one protocol that works on every device, every platform, every where.

Agent-Native

Email is structured, parsable, and conversational. Perfect for AI agents to read, understand, and act upon.

No Vendor Lock-in

Your data, your inbox, your rules. Clawederous works with any email provider. You're never trapped.

Instant Workflows

Shorthand syntax. Natural language. Email becomes your personal command bus for automation.
